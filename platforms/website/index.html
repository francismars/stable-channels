Deep dive

Step by step, use Lighning to keep stable USD value.

The two participants are as follows: The stableReceiver wants to keep a set amount of Bitcoin value stable in USD terms. The stableReceiver wants to be paid by a counterparty if the price of Bitcoin goes down in USD terms (i.e. it will take more Bitcoin to keep stable in USD terms).

How does it work?

The Stable Receiver is willing to pay a counterparty if the price of Bitcoin goes up in USD terms (i.e. it will take less Bitcoin to keep stable in USD terms) The Stable Provider is willing to provide that stability to the stableReceiver, most likely because he wants to be leveraged long Bitcoin.

The stableProvider is willing to pay a counterparty if the price of Bitcoin goes down against Bitcoin (it will take more Bitcoin to keep the stableReceiver stable in USD terms). The stableProvider is wants to be paid by a counterparty if the price of Bitcoin goes up against Bitcoin (it will take less Bitcoin to keep the stableReceiver stable in USD terms)

A few notes on the trade-offs here before we proceed: The stableReceiver forfeits the upside of her Bitcoin holdings. The stableProvider pays for any downside Bitcoin risk. The stableProvider takes the upside of the stableReceiver‚Äôs Bitcoin holdings

LN stables could accomplish this by settling price differences every minute. If a counterparty does not settle the difference after several attempts or, say, 0.5% price differential, the LN stable agreement is terminated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to commonly asked questions

What happens is the price goes down a lot?

In the base case, a balanced Stable Channels can tolerate a 50% drop in the price of bitcoin, and still keep the user stable.

What information do you collect?

We do not collect any usage or analytics information when you view this website or use Stable Channels on your computer. While it would be really helpful to understand how you are using the app, your privacy is far more important.

What are the system requirements?
  • Stable Channels is actively tested on Core Lightning (CLN) and LND. We plan to add other Lightning spec implementations.
  • Core Lightning or LND and Python are required to run Stable Channels.
Can I use it on mainnet or testnet?

Stable Channels was built to work on mainnet .

Is this service available to US citizens?

No. Sorry.

Can I contribute to the project?
  • If you use Stable Channels, then any feedback you have would be greatly appreciated.
  • If you want to stable USD value, you can use Stable Channels as a Stable Receiver.
  • If you want to go levered long BTC, you can use Stable Channels as a Stable Provider.
